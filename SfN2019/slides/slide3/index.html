Cell-type specific activity during human seizures


  • For population analyses, neurons from Utah array recordings were classified as putative PV+ interneurons or regular spiking cells (primarily pyramidal) using broadband spike FWHM and the cell-intrinsic mean autocorrelation lag
  • Previously, activity from these cells could not be tracked through ictal recruitment due to waveform changes—especially interneurons due to their smaller amplitude spikes

Population cross-correlations by cell-type

  1. As the wavefront approaches, interneuronal firing increases and shifts out of phase, confirming recent animal work (Miri et al., 2018)
  2. This is followed by increased pyramidal cell firing
  3. Maximal firing is during the wavefront, but both cell types maintain firing rates significantly above baseline
  4. Pyramidal cell firing continues to be reduced after interneuronal activity until tonic firing as the seizure invades


Evidence that interneuron firing increases only transiently, then decreases prior to pyramidal cells, suggesting a two-step process of seizure propagation
