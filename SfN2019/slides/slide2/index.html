Ictal recruitment of single neurons in human mesial temporal lobe (patient 15, seizure 2)

  • We used these metrics to differentiate between recruited and unrecruited tissue in Behnke-Fried recordings
  • The two types of neuronal behavior during seizures were evident in single unit activity in these recordings and corresponded well with the clinical determination of seizure onset & spread.
  • Recruitment could be detected using these methods without the need for a clear passage of the ictal wavefront in the MUA, which is not readily accessible in Behnke-Fried recordings
  • Waveshape changes occurred with a delay between sites, in keeping with propagation of ictal activity, and discounting motion or reference artifacts

Recruitment propagates between regions with a delay

  • In 7 seizures (21%) from 5 patients (24%) both types of neuronal activity occurred simultaneously at different locations with p < 0.05 in one location and ≥ 0.05 in another; Holm-Bonferroni corrected Mann-Whitney U test
  • This supports the concept of locally recruited neurons, with a penumbral response without waveshape changes in brain regions outside the recruited territory due to downstream excitatory effects
