Introduction

The intracellular hallmark of epileptic activity requires action potential alterations (Goldensohn & Purpura, 1963), though little evidence has been found in studies of single neurons in humans (Bower et al., 2012).

We developed a novel spike sorting method to overcome the inability to track neurons in seizing brain (Merricks et al., 2015) and analyzed a total of 1,239 single units from 41 seizures in 27 patients with medically intractable epilepsy (6 with Utah arrays, 21 with Behnke-Fried microwires) to determine extracellular correlates of these features & analyze cell-type specific activity through seizures.

Template matching with convex hulls

  • 3D convex hulls are built around pre-ictal units in principal component (PC) space. Scores are calculated for the ictal waveforms based on pre-ictal PCs, and waveforms that fit within these convex hulls are added as putative matches
  • Outliers were removed from the tail of a chi-squared distribution for the Mahalanobis distances for each unit
  • Waveform match probability was calculated by the Gaussian distributions of voltage through time of the original pre-ictal units
